"The Catch" - 1981 NFC Championship Game

Home > San Francisco 49ers > 1980s Dynasty

Game Details

Date
January 10, 1982
Location
Candlestick Park, San Francisco, California
Teams
San Francisco 49ers vs. Dallas Cowboys
Final Score
San Francisco 49ers 28, Dallas Cowboys 27
Championship
NFC Championship Game (led to Super Bowl XVI)

The Iconic Play

With 58 seconds left in the game and the 49ers trailing 27-21, quarterback Joe Montana threw a high pass to Dwight Clark in the back of the end zone. Clark leaped and made a fingertip catch to score the touchdown that, after the extra point, gave the 49ers a 28-27 victory.

This play, forever known as "The Catch," is considered one of the most famous plays in NFL history and marked the beginning of the 49ers dynasty of the 1980s. It also symbolized the changing of the guard in the NFC, with the 49ers replacing the Cowboys as the conference's dominant team.

Key Participants

  • Joe Montana (QB, 49ers) - Threw the pass
  • Dwight Clark (WR, 49ers) - Made the iconic catch
  • Bill Walsh - Designed the "Sprint Right Option" play

Historical Significance

The game marked the end of the Cowboys' dominance in the NFC and the beginning of the 49ers dynasty. The 49ers would go on to win Super Bowl XVI against the Cincinnati Bengals, the first of their five Super Bowl championships.

"The Catch" is considered the moment that launched the 49ers dynasty and established Joe Montana as one of the NFL's premier quarterbacks.
