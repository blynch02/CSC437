Game Summary

Super Bowl XVI was the first Super Bowl victory for the San Francisco 49ers. The 49ers built a 20-0 halftime lead and held off a second-half comeback by the Bengals. Joe Montana was named MVP after completing 14 of 22 passes for 157 yards and one touchdown, while also rushing for 18 yards and a touchdown.

The game featured a goal-line stand by the 49ers defense in the third quarter, stopping the Bengals on three consecutive plays from the one-yard line. This defensive stand is considered one of the greatest in Super Bowl history.
